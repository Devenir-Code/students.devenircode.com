Baked Fresh Daily.

Sourdough is an ancient method of leavening bread. It’s a slow process, and it produces large bubbles in the final dough, giving the bread its classic open crumb. We use a slow fermentation process to allow the dough to naturally develop our signature sourdough flavor over 24 hours, while retaining moisture and eliminating the need for preservatives. Our sourdough loaves are made with mineralized water and locally sourced salt.

The Process

1

Our naturally leavened sourdough bread is made using a sourdough starter for a unique tangy flavor and chewy texture.

2

We use only locally sourced and organic ingredients in our sourdough bread to support sustainable farming practices and promote a healthier environment.

3

Our sourdough bread undergoes a long fermentation process of up to 24 hours for a more flavorful and easily digestible loaf.

4

Our sourdough bread is baked using traditional artisanal techniques, resulting in a crusty exterior and airy interior that are both beautiful and delicious.

Organic & Local

Only the Best for Our Bread

Organic and locally sourced ingredients can make a big difference in the quality of our sourdough bread, not only in terms of taste and texture, but also in terms of supporting sustainable and ethical farming practices that benefit our local community and the environment. By using these ingredients, we are proud to offer our customers a healthier and more delicious bread while also making a positive impact.

Redmond salt is mined in Utah and contains a unique blend of natural minerals, including calcium, potassium, and magnesium, that can enhance the taste and nutritional value of our bread.

Using purified, mineralized water in our sourdough bread baking process helps ensure the best possible flavor and texture by removing impurities and adding essential minerals to the dough.

Using locally sourced, organic flour in our sourdough bread supports sustainable farming practices and results in a more flavorful, nutritious, and environmentally friendly product.
